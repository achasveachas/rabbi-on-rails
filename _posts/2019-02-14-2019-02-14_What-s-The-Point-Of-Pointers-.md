<h3 name="945a" id="945a" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">What’s The Point Of Pointers?</h3>
<p name="0399" id="0399" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">This Dvar Torah was originally published in Torah &amp;&amp; Tech, the weekly newsletter I publish together with my good friend </em><a href="https://medium.com/u/8b0c7fbdbada" data-href="https://medium.com/u/8b0c7fbdbada" data-anchor-type="2" data-user-id="8b0c7fbdbada" data-action-value="8b0c7fbdbada" data-action="show-user-card" data-action-type="hover" class="markup--user markup--p-user" target="_blank"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Ben Greenberg</em></a><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">. To get the weekly issue delivered straight to your inbox click </em><a href="https://mailchi.mp/f78e9b44f28d/torahandtech" data-href="https://mailchi.mp/f78e9b44f28d/torahandtech" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">here</em></a><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">.</em></p>
<figure name="d045" id="d045" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*TXyrr_5M_KCcLdxQ.png" data-width="360" data-height="299" data-is-featured="true" src="/assets/images/posts/2019-02-14-2019-02-14_What-s-The-Point-Of-Pointers--0.png"><figcaption class="imageCaption"><em class="markup--em markup--figure-em">Source: </em><a href="https://xkcd.com/138/" data-href="https://xkcd.com/138/" class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em class="markup--em markup--figure-em">XKCD — Pointers</em></a></figcaption></figure><p name="967a" id="967a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">This week’s Torah portion, <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Tetzaveh</em>, has the unusual distinction of being the only portion in the Torah, starting with his birth in <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Shemot</em>, where Moshe’s name is not mentioned even once.</p>
<p name="a797" id="a797" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The 13th-century commentary, the <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Ba’al Haturim</em>, gives an interesting explanation. After the Jews sinned with the Golden Calf, Moshe was begging G-D to forgive them, saying “Now, if You will forgive their sin [well and good]; but if not, erase me from the book which You have written!” (Shemot 32:32).</p>
<p name="c05a" id="c05a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Ba’al Haturim</em> goes on to explain: “Although this exclamation was conditional upon God’s refusal to forgive the people for the sin of the Calf, the curse of a scholar is fulfilled in some way, even when made conditionally.” So even though G-D ended up forgiving the Jewish people and didn’t have to go through with Moshe’s “threat,” he nevertheless went through with it to a smaller extent by removing Moses’s name from one portion of the Torah.</p>
<p name="d21e" id="d21e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The Lubavitcher Rebbe <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">OBM</em> used to point out a, seemingly ironic, fact. Even though G-D removed Moshe’s name from the Torah portion, Moshe himself was not. The portion of Tetzaveh contains numerous references to Moshe in the first person, starting with the very first verse, “And <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">you</strong> shall instruct the Jewish People…”</p>
<p name="1920" id="1920" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">In a certain sense, the Rebbe explained, Moshe’s presence in <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Tetzaveh</em> is actually felt much stronger. Where a name is external, only used by others to address us, “you” refers to your essence. In other words, in “erasing” Moshe’s name from the Torah, G-D wrote in Moshe “himself.”</p>
<p name="356d" id="356d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Though it may seem counter to Moshe’s intent, it was actually pretty fitting. In Moshe’s declaration “erase me from the book which You have written” Moshe revealed his true essence as the ultimate leader, willing to put his life’s work on the line to save his people.</p>
<p name="5415" id="5415" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">What does all of this have to do with tech? I’m (<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Yechiel</strong>) glad you asked (and I would like to apologize if this part gets more technical than previous newsletters).</p>
<p name="e90d" id="e90d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Being relatively new to the Go language, one of the features that confused me a lot at first was the idea of pointers. Coming from Ruby and Python where pointers don’t exist I had a hard time wrapping my head around them and why they were necessary. Why is a pointer to a location in memory where a value is stored any different than a variable that holds that value? I believe the idea above, about the difference between Moshe’s name and Moshe himself, can help us understand.</p>
<p name="8d6e" id="8d6e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Consider the following code sample:</p>
<pre name="4a1c" id="4a1c" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p"><code class="markup--code markup--pre-code">func increment(num int) {<br> num+=1<br>}</code></pre>
<pre name="404b" id="404b" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--pre"><code class="markup--code markup--pre-code">func main(){<br> var x = 1<br> increment(x)<br> println(x)<br>}</code></pre>
<p name="4db6" id="4db6" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre"><a href="https://play.golang.org/p/fN1qIZD8Ycw" data-href="https://play.golang.org/p/fN1qIZD8Ycw" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Playground link</a></p>
<p name="1b3e" id="1b3e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">We have a function <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">increment()</code> that takes a number and increments it by 1. We then take a variable <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">x</code>, assign it to the number 1, we call <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">increment</code> on <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">x</code>and then print it. The result is that gets printed is not 2 (the result of incrementing <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">x</code> by 1); instead, 1 gets printed.</p>
<p name="ffe5" id="ffe5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The reason for this behavior is that when we call <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">increment(x)</code>, the increment function makes a copy of the value of <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">x</code>, assigns it to a new variable in its own scope, and increments <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">that</strong>. The original <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">x</code> remains unchanged.</p>
<p name="01f5" id="01f5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">If we wanted <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">increment()</code> to increment the actual value of <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">x</code> we could modify our code as follows:</p>
<pre name="c8a5" id="c8a5" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p"><code class="markup--code markup--pre-code">func increment(num *int) {<br> *num+=1<br>}</code></pre>
<pre name="0187" id="0187" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--pre"><code class="markup--code markup--pre-code">func main() {<br> var x = 1<br> increment(&amp;x)<br> println(x)<br>}</code></pre>
<p name="2809" id="2809" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre"><a href="https://play.golang.org/p/dox_cvSjuQA" data-href="https://play.golang.org/p/dox_cvSjuQA" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Playground link</a></p>
<p name="d37d" id="d37d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Our new <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">increment()</code> doesn't take a number as an argument, it takes a pointer to a number, an address in memory where that number is stored. And when <code class="markup--code markup--p-code">increment()</code> increments the number, it increments the number at that location in memory. Running the second code snippet gives us 2 as we expect. All because we pointed at the number itself instead of at its value.</p>
<p name="5abc" id="5abc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Sometimes, in life, we have to know where to point to and what defines the mark we leave around us and what actually defines us, in our essence.</p>
