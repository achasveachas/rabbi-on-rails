<h3 name="0f86" id="0f86" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">Of Scaffolds and Gems</h3>
<p name="266f" id="266f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Throughout my coding journey I have reached various milestones; <a href="https://blog.yechiel.me/program-0-1790417e5313" data-href="https://blog.yechiel.me/program-0-1790417e5313" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">my first computer program</a>, <a href="https://rubygems.org/gems/dansdeals" data-href="https://rubygems.org/gems/dansdeals" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">my first published gem</a>, and my first fully functioning bot capable of playing <a href="https://blog.yechiel.me/tic-tac-tips-517d5c80f47d" data-href="https://blog.yechiel.me/tic-tac-tips-517d5c80f47d" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Tic Tac Toe</a>.</p>
<p name="c71b" id="c71b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Recently I reached another milestone. My first contribution to an open source project was accepted and published. I guess I managed to convince someone else that I know how to code as well :)</p>
<p name="cf76" id="cf76" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It all started when I finished the section on Sinatra in <a href="http://learn.co/with/achasveachas" data-href="http://learn.co/with/achasveachas" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Learn</a>’s curriculum. At the end of the section we are expected to build our own Sinatra app from scratch. I have <a href="https://blog.yechiel.me/gem-install-swim-82ff7a95e605" data-href="https://blog.yechiel.me/gem-install-swim-82ff7a95e605" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">blogged</a> in past about how sometimes being thrown into the deep-end is the best way to learn how to swim, and somehow this time it felt a little less scary.</p>
<p name="9190" id="9190" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The hardest part of every project is to take the first step, and here too I was at a loss how to go about it. Throughout the curriculum we pass different labs and challenges, and what all of them have in common is that the basic structure for the app is provided for us. This time I was staring at a blank screen not knowing where to start.</p>
<p name="938b" id="938b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Back when I was making my CLI gem, I learned there was a powerful tool for generating the basic structure of a gem called Bundler, if I could just find something similar for Sinatra apps I would be on my way.</p>
<p name="154a" id="154a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Naturally I turned to my trusted mentor, Google.</p>
<figure name="9dd0" id="9dd0" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*jipIiMS2xdIv6_M0IQBtMg.gif" data-width="657" data-height="352" src="/assets/images/posts/2016-08-28-2016-08-28_Of-Scaffolds-and-Gems-0.gif"></figure><p name="2c99" id="2c99" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">This time however Google wasn’t much help. It didn’t look like there was such a thing.</p>
<p name="9e0a" id="9e0a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">So I dug around a bit more, asked around on Learn’s Slack channel and found that our very own <a href="http://http://www.brianemory.com/" data-href="http://http://www.brianemory.com/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Brian Emory</a>, a fellow Learn graduate, created just what I was looking for, a nifty gem called <a href="https://rubygems.org/gems/corneal" data-href="https://rubygems.org/gems/corneal" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Corneal</a> that generates the basic structure for a Sinatra app.</p>
<figure name="66e7" id="66e7" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*UyGyw4z4YlRptP3KtY8xCA.png" data-is-featured="true" src="/assets/images/posts/2016-08-28-2016-08-28_Of-Scaffolds-and-Gems-1.png"></figure><p name="1e29" id="1e29" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">So I spent some time playing around with Sinatra, getting utterly impressed that this was all made by a fellow student, and then that got me thinking; if a fellow student made such a cool gem, knowing not much more than what I did, what would stop me from adding improvements to the gem?</p>
<p name="5bcc" id="5bcc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">There was a feature in the gem (added by another fellow Learner) that added Ruby Models to the app, I decided to take it a step further and add a feature that would add the Model along with its associated erb views and Sinatra controllers, similar, as I would later learn, to Rails’ Scaffold generator.</p>
<p name="e4b0" id="e4b0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It took 2 all-nighters during which I delved into Thor documentation, collaborated with a fellow Learner in Nigeria, and spent hours getting frustrated by a bug in our IDE, but finally my feature was ready. I submitted the pull request and not long after was notified that my feature was accepted!</p>
<figure name="bcd5" id="bcd5" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*ePbfjMq_3r3aOEr2.jpg" data-width="660" data-height="606" src="/assets/images/posts/2016-08-28-2016-08-28_Of-Scaffolds-and-Gems-2.jpg"></figure><p name="cae3" id="cae3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">My first reaction was severe impostor syndrome “Oh my gosh! He published it! What will happen when everyone will realize I had no clue what I was doing and the whole thing crashes?!” but eventually I realized that that was all part of the process, it’s all part of the learning and the more real world experience I would be gaining, the closer I would get to the day when I was creating stuff all on my own!</p>
<p name="e5e7" id="e5e7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">Happy coding!</p>
