<p name="2136" id="2136" class="graf graf--p graf--leading"><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Last week, after the weekly Torah &amp;&amp; Tech newsletter went out, we were saddened to hear about the passing of Ben’s father, Dan Pesach ben Baruch Shmuel Hakohen Greenberg. This week’s issue is dedicated to his memory.</em></p>
<figure name="f688" id="f688" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*YeZoInjGmGKwDYNY" data-width="500" data-height="300" data-is-featured="true" alt="Yaakov fighting with the angel" src="/assets/images/posts/2019-12-13-2019-12-13_It-s-All-In-The-Details-0.jpg"></figure><p name="4d15" id="4d15" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">In this week’s Torah portion, Parshat Vayishlach, we read how Yaakov, who, after spending 21 years in Charan in the home of his uncle Lavan, was now on his way home to the Land of Israel.</p>
<p name="f6e0" id="f6e0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">On the way, the Torah describes how Yaakov took his family and belongings across the Yabok river. The Torah then describes how “Yaakov was left alone, and a man wrestled with him until dawn.” (<em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Bereshis 32:25</em>)</p>
<p name="ea9f" id="ea9f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Why was Yaakov on his own? The Talmud (<em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Chulin 91a</em>) says that Yaakov went back across the river in the middle of the night to retrieve some “small jugs” that were left behind.</p>
<p name="b6f3" id="b6f3" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">From this, the Talmud learns the value of even little, seemingly insignificant things. Even a few “little jugs” weren’t too insignificant for him, and he refused to leave them behind.</p>
<p name="7981" id="7981" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">As developers, we are all too painfully aware of the importance of the little things.</p>
<p name="8c23" id="8c23" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">How many times have you spent time hunting down a bug that turned out to be a misplaced closing bracket or a forgotten capitalization?</p>
<p name="02a4" id="02a4" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">What we need to keep in mind is that the “little things” count in our interpersonal relationships as well. In our interactions with our teammates and co-workers.</p>
<p name="51ca" id="51ca" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">We have no idea what a good morning said with a smile and a genuine interest in the well-being of a teammate who seems to be burning out can accomplish.</p>
<p name="2648" id="2648" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Let us try our best to pay attention to the little things, the ones that make the biggest difference!</p>
<p name="fd0c" id="fd0c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Shabbat Shalom!</p>
