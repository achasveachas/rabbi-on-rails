<h3 name="20f6" id="20f6" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">Talmud and Code</h3>
<p name="971f" id="971f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Someone recently shared an awesome speech with me. It’s a keynote by Yitzchak Willroth (A.K.A <a href="https://twitter.com/coderabbi" data-href="https://twitter.com/coderabbi" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">coderabbi</a>) given at Midwest.io 2015</p>
<figure name="1a77" id="1a77" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*5qBX-6mTzWG0I8MJHmyX8Q.jpeg" data-width="640" data-height="568" src="/assets/images/posts/2016-09-14-2016-09-14_Talmud-and-Code-0.jpeg"></figure><p name="a152" id="a152" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">The talk is titled <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Talmudic Maxims to Maximize Your Growth as a Developer, </strong>subtitled <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Everything that I really needed to know I learned in rabbinical school.</strong></p>
<p name="5080" id="5080" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The talk is fascinating. Rabbi Willroth, who is an accomplished PHP developer, shares many lessons he learned during his years studying in Yeshiva, and how he applied them to his career as a programmer.</p>
<p name="ee4b" id="ee4b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Famous Talmudic adages are cast in a new light. So <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Cultivate for yourself a mentor and acquire for yourself a colleague </strong><em class="markup--em markup--p-em">(Avos 1:6)</em><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong"> </strong>is actually advice for us to apprentice by someone who can mentor us to improve ourselves, while at the same time taking time to get some pair-coding with others and learn from them.</p>
<p name="dc06" id="dc06" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">From my students I have learned most of all</strong> (<em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Ta’anis 7a</em>) teaches us to always try to teach others, not only because it will benefit <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">them</strong> but because it will benefit <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">you. </strong>The best way to learn, the sages teach us, is by teaching others.</p>
