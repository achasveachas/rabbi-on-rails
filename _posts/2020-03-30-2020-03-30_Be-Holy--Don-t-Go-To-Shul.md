<h3 name="4ac3" id="4ac3" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">Be Holy, Don’t Go To Shul</h3>

<figure name="0324" id="0324" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*v7sXDqiCOKl11tLG.jpg" data-width="650" data-height="245" data-is-featured="true" alt="Hasidim Dancing" src="/assets/images/posts/2020-03-30-2020-03-30_Be-Holy--Don-t-Go-To-Shul-0.jpg"></figure><p name="4fe5" id="4fe5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">It’s a painful irony that in the week where we learn the Torah portion of Vayakhel (“and [Moshe] gathered [the Jewish People]”), most Jews are confining themselves to their homes. Of course, they are doing it to protect themselves and their loved ones from the spread of the coronavirus that’s currently putting the world on lock-down.</p>
<p name="dcdc" id="dcdc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">With many Shuls, temples, mosques, churches, etc. suspending services, I’m sure many people who rely on that spiritual connection are feeling pretty down.</p>
<p name="f332" id="f332" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s time to reflect on one of my favorite Chassidic stories:</p>
<p name="ebc1" id="ebc1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Reb Zushe and Reb Elimelech (who’s Yahrtzeit was this past Tuesday, the 21st of Adar) were two brothers who lived in Ukraine in the 1700s and were among the earliest leaders of the Chassidic movement.</p>
<p name="8e23" id="8e23" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">They would often dress like peasants and travel from town to town without anyone knowing their real identity (this was in the pre-Instagram days when it was still possible).</p>
<p name="7e74" id="7e74" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">One time they arrived at a village and stayed at the local inn.</p>
<p name="ab7d" id="ab7d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Overnight, some expensive cutlery went missing, and suspicion naturally fell on the two unknown “peasants.”</p>
<p name="ee59" id="ee59" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The distraught innkeeper called the police. The brothers tried to explain their innocence, but the police naturally took the word of the innkeeper over the word of the two “unknowns.” To make a long story short, the two brothers soon found themselves in a cell surrounded by the lowest elements of Ukrainian society.</p>
<p name="0654" id="0654" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">In the morning, Reb Elimelech was getting ready to say the morning prayers, but Reb Zushe pointed out to him that there was a problem. The “problem” took the form of a bucket in the corner of the cell that was there for the inmates to relieve themselves. Jewish law forbids praying in a room with human excrement, so as long as the bucket was there, praying would not be possible.</p>
<p name="7588" id="7588" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Reb Elimelech was extremely disappointed! In his entire life, he never missed a day’s prayers! It was inconceivable to him not to pray even for one day! He started crying.</p>
<p name="d2b8" id="d2b8" class="graf graf--p graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--p">“Why are you crying?” asked his brother, “in the same way that Halachah tells us to pray every day, Halachah also tells us <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">not</strong> to pray in a situation like ours!”</p>
<p name="32f7" id="32f7" class="graf graf--p graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--p">“Every day, we get the opportunity to serve G-d by praying, today we will have the opportunity to serve G-d by <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">not</strong> praying!”</p>
<p name="12eb" id="12eb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Reb Elimelech heard what his brother had to say, realized the truth in his words, and thanked him for his comforting thoughts.</p>
<p name="c312" id="c312" class="graf graf--p graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--p">“Furthermore,” said Reb Zusha, “there is a Mitzvah to serve G-d with joy. Every day we fulfill it by praying joyfully; today, we will fulfill it by <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">not</strong> praying with equal joy!”</p>
<p name="5389" id="5389" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The two brothers set out to serve God in this novel way, and they started singing a joyful melody. Before long, they forgot about their troubles and their surroundings and started dancing right there in the cell surrounded by their non-Jewish cellmates.</p>
<p name="31ed" id="31ed" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The cellmates, initially thought the Jews had gone mad, but they welcomed the break in the monotony and soon joined in the dancing. Before long, the entire cell was one big dance party!</p>
<p name="9a96" id="9a96" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">When the guard heard the commotion from outside, he came running in. He grabbed the nearest prisoner and asked what all the dancing was about? “This is a jail, not a nightclub!” he yelled.</p>
<p name="12c4" id="12c4" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The poor prisoner couldn’t say much; he didn’t really know himself! All he knew was that those two crazy Jews were pointing at the toilet bucket, started crying, then they pointed at it some more, and then they started singing and dancing!</p>
<p name="2bc8" id="2bc8" class="graf graf--p graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--p">“Aha!” exclaimed the sadistic guard, “so the bucket is what’s making them so happy? I can take care of that!”</p>
<p name="7835" id="7835" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">He ran to the corner, grabbed the bucket, and with a sneering look at the two brothers, he took it out of the cell.</p>
<p name="2e01" id="2e01" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Reb Zushe turned to his brother, “Elimelech, my brother! Now we can pray!”</p>
<p name="f8e5" id="f8e5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">So as you sit at home this Shabbat and pray in whichever way you find meaningful, rememeber, you are staying home to keep yourself and your community safe! There is no greater Mitzvah than the Mitzvah to protect another life!</p>
<p name="f451" id="f451" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Sure, missing out on that human interaction is hard, but the Jewish people have been through worse! At least this time, it’s our love for our fellow community members that is keeping us from going to Shul, and not the hatred of our enemies.</p>
<p name="4843" id="4843" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Shabbat Shalom and stay safe and healthy!</p>
<p name="a454" id="a454" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">Yechiel</p>
