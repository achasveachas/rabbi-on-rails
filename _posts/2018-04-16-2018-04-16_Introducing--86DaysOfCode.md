<h3 name="a08d" id="a08d" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">Introducing <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/86DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/86DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--h3-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#86DaysOfCode</a>
</h3>
<h4 name="ba7d" id="ba7d" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--h3 graf--subtitle">Can Less Be More? A New Approach To The 100 Days Of Code Challenge</h4>
<figure name="43ad" id="43ad" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--h4"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*dltyp0sr5P6eVcF4T1RWxQ.png" data-width="885" data-height="199" src="/assets/images/posts/2018-04-16-2018-04-16_Introducing--86DaysOfCode-0.png"></figure><p name="599b" id="599b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Anyone hanging out in Coding Twitter is by now familiar with the <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#100DaysOfCode</a> hashtag.</p>
<p name="ab58" id="ab58" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The hashtag is full of inspirational stories. For those who don’t know, <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#100DaysOfCode</a> is a challenge that people challenge themselves to spend some time coding every single day for 100 days straight.</p>
<p name="5e78" id="5e78" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It’s a great way to develop a habit; do anything for 100 days straight, and you’ll find it hard to stop. Even I took a shot at the challenge:</p>
<figure name="3485" id="3485" class="graf graf--figure graf--iframe graf-after--p"><blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><a href="https://twitter.com/yechielk/status/833852475753037824"></a></blockquote>
<script async src="https://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></figure><p name="1fab" id="1fab" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Recently, however, I noticed a few <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#100DaysOfCode</a> posts that got me thinking.</p>
<p name="5f5d" id="5f5d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Every once in a while I came across people who missed a day or two, and that usually caused some anxiety. Is the challenge finished? Did they fail? Do they need to start over? Is it okay to “cheat” and keep going?</p>
<p name="a0f2" id="a0f2" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Seeing such questions made me sad. The 100 Days Of Code challenge is about building good habits; beating yourself up over a missed day is not a good habit to get into.</p>
<p name="e5fb" id="e5fb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That train of thought got me thinking further. How about expecting yourself to code for 100 days straight? Is that a habit worth encouraging? What about taking time for yourself? Allowing yourself to have an off-day? Taking time for family? Aren’t those worth anything?</p>
<h4 name="0e44" id="0e44" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">A Better Way?</h4>
<p name="b719" id="b719" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4">Back when I was taking the online web-development course at The <a href="https://medium.com/u/973c5cbfb09b" data-href="https://medium.com/u/973c5cbfb09b" data-anchor-type="2" data-user-id="973c5cbfb09b" data-action-value="973c5cbfb09b" data-action="show-user-card" data-action-type="hover" class="markup--user markup--p-user" target="_blank">Flatiron School</a>, I had a crazy schedule. I still had a full-time job, a family, and my coding that were all competing for my attention.</p>
<p name="cdb0" id="cdb0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">One of the ways I managed to maintain my sanity was by reserving one day a week for myself/family during which I didn’t go near the computer. It was my day off, the day that allowed me to recharge so I can tackle the week of full work days followed by sleepless nights spent with code.</p>
<h4 name="db78" id="db78" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">Introducing <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/86DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/86DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--h4-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#86DaysOfCode</a>
</h4>
<p name="5457" id="5457" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4">Thinking back to those days, I started wondering, is it possible to build time for yourself into the <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#100DaysOfCode</a> challenge? What if instead of <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/100DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#100DaysOfCode</a> we would try to do <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/86DaysOfCode" data-href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/86DaysOfCode" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">#86DaysOfCode</a> letting everyone take off one day a week to take care of themselves? I’m sure that would help boost productivity in the other six days of the week so the 14 missing days would make up for themselves 100-fold.</p>
<p name="78df" id="78df" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">What do you think?</p>
