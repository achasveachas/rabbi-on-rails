<div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><h3 name="5622" id="5622" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">The Privilege Of Learning</h3></div>
<div class="section-inner sectionLayout--outsetColumn"><figure name="85db" id="85db" class="graf graf--figure graf--layoutOutsetCenter graf-after--h3"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*ipO_y93PfBik2w8N3lHtaQ.jpeg" data-width="1180" data-height="599" src="/assets/images/posts/2017-10-24-2017-10-24_The-Privilege-Of-Learning-0.jpeg"></figure></div>
<div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn">
<p name="b476" id="b476" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">I recently came across a <a href="https://www.quora.com/Why-dont-more-poor-people-with-internet-access-teach-themselves-to-code-and-earn-a-more-comfortable-living-What-other-barriers-may-be-in-play-here/answer/Yechiel-Kay?srid=RQhO" data-href="https://www.quora.com/Why-dont-more-poor-people-with-internet-access-teach-themselves-to-code-and-earn-a-more-comfortable-living-What-other-barriers-may-be-in-play-here/answer/Yechiel-Kay?srid=RQhO" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">question</a> on Quora that got me nervous, mainly because of the underlying assumptions. I figured I would cross post it here in case anyone here can benefit from it:</p>
<h4 name="c1d2" id="c1d2" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p"><a href="https://www.quora.com/Why-dont-more-poor-people-with-internet-access-teach-themselves-to-code-and-earn-a-more-comfortable-living-What-other-barriers-may-be-in-play-here" data-href="https://www.quora.com/Why-dont-more-poor-people-with-internet-access-teach-themselves-to-code-and-earn-a-more-comfortable-living-What-other-barriers-may-be-in-play-here" class="markup--anchor markup--h4-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><strong class="markup--strong markup--h4-strong">Why don’t more poor people with internet access teach themselves to code, and earn a more comfortable living? What other barriers may be in play here?</strong></a></h4>
<p name="1528" id="1528" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4">I believe I am qualified to answer this question. Let me tell you a bit about myself, and you will see why.</p>
<p name="086e" id="086e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">About two years ago we were at a low point. Our landlord notified us that he would not be renewing the lease, and rent in our area was too high for us to afford.</p>
<p name="9cb4" id="9cb4" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">My wife was out of a job, and one income was just not enough to make rent on even a smaller apartment. Without much choices, we ended up moving with our three children into my parents’ basement, where we stayed for about a year.</p>
<p name="ab19" id="ab19" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">At around that time (actually it started a bit before we found out we’d be homeless soon), I started looking into upgrading my skill set. Programming was a field I always enjoyed, and everyone said I’d be good at.</p>
<p name="707c" id="707c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The problem was that to learn enough to be career ready, in a short enough time to make it practical I’d have to look into some bootcamp-style program, and those are: A) expensive and B) involve taking three months+ off work which I just could not afford.</p>
<p name="6c1e" id="6c1e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Thankfully, around that time, I came across <a href="https://medium.com/u/973c5cbfb09b" data-href="https://medium.com/u/973c5cbfb09b" data-anchor-type="2" data-user-id="973c5cbfb09b" data-action-value="973c5cbfb09b" data-action="show-user-card" data-action-type="hover" class="markup--user markup--p-user" target="_blank">Flatiron School</a>’s online program. Now I had an option that was considerably cheaper, and, being online, meant I could do it part-time while keeping my job.</p>
<p name="e854" id="e854" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">With the help of a local organization that provided a scholarship for part of the tuition and a friend of my father who loaned me the rest under very comfortable “pay-me-when-you-can” terms, I was able to enroll in the program.</p>
<p name="4bc9" id="4bc9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That started a long hard year where I kept my full-time job during the day and stayed up coding all night (as late as 2–3am at least 3–4 nights a week) and on the weekends. During that year I also had to deal with the move into my parents’ basement as mentioned (and that includes supporting a wife who had to move into her mother-in-law’s basement).</p>
<p name="e778" id="e778" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It definitely wasn’t easy, but recently I finally graduated.</p>
<p name="b296" id="b296" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Things are looking better now, we recently moved into our own apartment, I was able to pay back the loan to my dad’s friend, my wife got a job, and I’m now interviewing for jobs where the starting salary is almost double what I’m currently making.</p>
<p name="04f1" id="04f1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">At first glance, my story seems to be a perfect illustration of what the OP was asking. A case of a “poor person” who was able to use the magic pill of teaching himself to code to get out of a tight situation, and give his family options.</p>
<p name="7a72" id="7a72" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">A deeper look at the details will show why that is not the case.</p>
<p name="5434" id="5434" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">For now, I will ignore the well-established debate regarding if everyone CAN learn to code (and enough to use it to make a living), and assume for argument’s sake that that’s an option for everyone.</p>
<p name="e9ab" id="e9ab" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I’ll be honest; I used to think like OP as well, I used to say “if I could do it, why can’t everyone?” But the more I’ve spoken to others in my situation, the more I realized that my situation was actually privileged (not counting the fact that I happen to live in a part of the world where internet access is common, OP hinted at that in the question). Let me highlight some of them:</p>
<p name="8d05" id="8d05" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Finance</strong>:</p>
<p name="dd47" id="dd47" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">As much as I complained about my financial situation in the beginning of this answer, we were still fortunate to have an extensive safety net. Many people don’t have parents capable and willing to open up their home to their grown-up children and most people don’t have friends who would loan them thousands of dollars like my father’s friend did. If not for those two factors (and others) there is no way I would have been able to afford, even the cheaper online option.</p>
<p name="4b21" id="4b21" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Peace Of Mind:</strong></p>
<p name="37b7" id="37b7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I’m fortunate to work at a 9–5 office job that doesn’t follow me home. Many people have much harder day jobs, by the time they get home they are wiped. Staying up until three on a regular basis, doing the type of heavy thinking needed to learn to code is an impossible request for them. I mean, even under the same conditions as me, not everyone can simply function on 4–5 hours of sleep on a regular basis.</p>
<p name="ddb5" id="ddb5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Support:</strong></p>
<p name="464f" id="464f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Luckily I had the full support of my wife (who was going through hard times herself). Not everyone has that luxury. Some people cannot rely on the support of their family, and may actually need to support <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">them</strong>.</p>
<p name="ce94" id="ce94" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I’m sure there are others, and every once in a while I think of different ways in which I’ve been blessed. But the main thing I learned is that I can never judge another’s situation. Implying that someone is poor because of their laziness or lack of will to improve their situation means that you fundamentally misunderstand what it means to be poor.</p>
<p name="6195" id="6195" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">My biggest hope is that I never forget these lessons and that when I’m in the situation where I can help those in my previous situation, I’ll grab that opportunity with both hands.</p>
<p name="97a9" id="97a9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing">Until then, if there’s anyone who is thinking of, or who started out on the path to code, and who is looking for someone to talk to, please feel free to reach out to me, and I will try to help to the best of my abilities.</p>
